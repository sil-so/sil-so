FlowTube is a free Chrome extension designed for Webflow developers and agencies to simplify working with exported code. Instead of manually unzipping, selecting files, and pushing projects to GitHub, FlowTube automates the process: every Webflow export is uploaded to your repository through a secure pipeline.

It removes repetitive file management tasks so you can focus on designing and building. No more juggling ZIPs, no more manual uploads, just a direct connection between Webflow and version control.

Key Features
  • Automatic detection of Webflow ZIPs with one-click GitHub uploads
  • File control: include/exclude CSS, JS, HTML, and set custom paths
  • Secure: GitHub tokens encrypted with AES-256-GCM, sent via HTTPS

FlowTube makes Webflow-to-GitHub handoffs consistent, reliable, and effortless.

Warp Domains is an AI-powered web app that helps makers, startups, and agencies find distinctive, available domain names. Users receive six personalized suggestions based on their pitch, with the ability to refine results by rating, saving top picks as “Gems,” and organizing everything in a private directory.

Each domain can be checked against 500+ TLDs directly in the interface, making availability checks effortless.

Key Features
  • AI-driven domain name generation
  • Rating and favorites system (“Gems”)
  • Availability checks across 500+ TLDs
  • Private directory for saved ideas
  • User accounts, credits, and multiple pricing plans

Warp Domains was originally built in a two-week SaaS competition and has since grown into a live beta platform for sharper, more efficient domain naming.

Curatyy™ is an early preview project exploring how AI can deepen our understanding of visual art. Instead of generating new images, it analyzes existing works to highlight subject, composition, mood, technique, and narrative elements.

At the moment, Curatyy™ exists as a CustomGPT on ChatGPT, serving as a testbed for how creators and art-lovers might use AI as a personal curator. By uploading or describing an artwork, you can explore new perspectives and uncover hidden layers of meaning.

What's Coming
  • Save and revisit analyses
  • Build a personal reference library
  • Curate and share galleries with others

Unlike many AI tools focused on producing art, Curatyy™ is designed for what comes after: helping you interpret, refine, and elevate creative work.

Van Drunen Woondecoratie is a family-run interior and window decoration business with deep local roots in Raamsdonksveer. Known for their personal service, craftsmanship, and trusted reputation built over generations, the goal for this project was to translate that sense of tradition and expertise into a contemporary online presence. The site needed to feel familiar to their loyal customers while being fresh and intuitive for new visitors.

Process & Approach

The new website was designed in Figma and developed in Webflow, following the Client-First framework by Finsweet for a clean, scalable structure. A CMS integration allows the team to easily update collections, product categories, and news items without technical assistance.

To preserve the brand’s recognizable identity, the design carefully builds on the existing visual language. It maintains the trusted tone while refining it for a modern digital environment. Custom animations and subtle interactions bring a sense of flow and craftsmanship to the browsing experience. Components from the Relume Library supported rapid prototyping, while custom code and a make.com automation were implemented to integrate Google reCAPTCHA v3, protecting the contact forms from spam submissions.

Brand & Visuals

Photography played a key role in conveying authenticity and warmth. All images were shot on a Fujifilm DSLR and refined in Adobe Lightroom, balancing natural light and soft tones to capture the inviting atmosphere of the showroom and the genuine connection between the people behind the brand.

Result

Launched in October 2023, the new website combines a timeless look with modern usability. It feels approachable and genuine, much like the experience of visiting the showroom itself. The result is a digital reflection of Van Drunen’s philosophy: real people, real craftsmanship, and thoughtful interior advice that stands the test of time.

Stottercentrum Rotterdam is a specialized speech therapy practice in the heart of Rotterdam, focused on helping children, teens, and adults who stutter or clutter. The project’s goal was to create a warm, trustworthy, and professional online presence that reflects the practice’s expertise while offering visitors an immediate sense of calm and confidence.

Process & Approach

The entire website was designed in Figma and built in Webflow, following the Client-First framework by Finsweet for structured, scalable development. Components from the Relume Library helped speed up prototyping and maintain visual consistency throughout the site.

Webflow CMS was integrated to make content management effortless. This allows the team at Stottercentrum Rotterdam to easily update therapy information, blog posts, and frequently asked questions. A make.com automation routes e-mail form submissions to multiple recipients, ensuring smooth communication between the practice’s specialists.

Brand & Visuals

The new visual identity, designed in Affinity Designer, is clean, approachable, and rooted in a soft green to convey reassurance and balance. Custom photography, shot entirely on an iPhone and edited in Adobe Lightroom, adds authenticity and a personal touch to the brand. The images capture both the inviting interior and the approachable atmosphere of the practice.

Result

The website went live in March 2023 and now serves as both a digital front door and an informative resource for people seeking help with stuttering or cluttering. It combines accessibility with a professional, human-centered design. What could have been a clinical experience has become something personal, supportive, and hopeful.
